# Precise Warmth

## When to Use

Load this skill automatically when working on any creative or design project (UI, video, animation, typography, branding, architecture, hardware). Also load when the user mentions keywords: aesthetics, design style, visual taste, art direction, creative direction, look and feel.

## Core Aesthetic

**A solitary individual moving with precise, restrained warmth through a vast, highly structured, technological system.**

This is not cold nihilism. It is warmth that is exact, rhythmic, directional — like an engine running at steady speed on a highway, not a candle flickering in ruins.

## Design Principles

1. **Minimalism over decoration.** Every element must earn its place. Remove until nothing remains that can be removed without breaking function.

2. **Geometric order.** Symmetry, grids, alignment. Structure is beauty. Asymmetry is acceptable only when the tension is intentional.

3. **Restrained palette.** Black, white, grayscale, and low-saturation natural tones. High saturation is an accent, never the foundation.

4. **Industrial honesty.** Expose the material truth — concrete, steel, glass, wood. No simulation of one material with another. No ornament that conceals structure.

5. **Negative space as a first-class element.** What is absent matters as much as what is present. Breathing room is not waste.

6. **Typography-first.** When in doubt, let type carry the composition. Monospaced and geometric sans-serif are the default voices. Typography should be crisp, intentional, never decorative.

7. **Terminal as interface.** The command line, the monospaced grid, the raw text surface — these are not limitations but aesthetic choices. Data presented without visual mediation is beautiful.

8. **Precise warmth.** Emotion is present but delivered through controlled, measured means — vocoder-filtered rather than belted raw. Sentiment should be earned, never forced.

9. **Agency in structure.** The individual has will and direction within the system. The aesthetic should convey forward motion, not stasis or surrender.

## Domain Guidance

### Visual / Graphic Design

- Favor Swiss/International Style: grid systems, sans-serif, asymmetric layouts within a grid
- Photography: wide-angle, elevated perspective, geometric composition, documentary detachment
- Information graphics: data-forward, minimal embellishment, the structure is the aesthetic
- Avoid: ornament, decoration for its own sake, sentimental imagery, excessive saturation

### UI / Digital Interfaces

- Light backgrounds with minimal color accents (Linear-inspired) or dark terminal aesthetics
- Monospaced fonts for data, code, and functional interfaces
- Geometric sans-serif for labels and headings
- Sparse use of color — one accent color is often enough
- Maltego-style dark graph/network UIs are a valid reference
- Functional density is acceptable; decorative density is not
- Hardware-inspired UI elements (HUD overlays, terminal chrome) are welcome
- Avoid: rounded corners as default, gradients as decoration, Material Design warmth, skeuomorphism

### Architecture / Spatial Design

- Modernist/ Bauhaus: form follows function, honest materials, no applied ornament
- Brutalism: exposed concrete, bold massing, monumental scale
- Geometric layouts, clean sightlines, industrial lighting
- Ideal workspace: concrete walls, steel shelving, solid wood desk, industrial task lamp, city skyline, everything in its place
- Avoid: overly cozy, cluttered, decorative traditional, faux-historical

### Film / Moving Image

- Fixed or slow-moving camera, locked-off wide shots
- Symmetric composition, geometric framing
- Cool or neutral color grading, controlled contrast
- Long takes, patient editing rhythm
- Environmental storytelling: the space tells the story as much as the characters
- Documentary-style handheld when intimacy is needed
- Avoid: rapid cutting for energy, handheld shake for faux realism, warm golden-hour everything

### Sound / Music Direction

- Electronic foundation: synthesizers, drum machines, vocoders, processed sound
- Rhythmic drive, forward momentum, steady pulse
- Precision over rawness — quantized, processed, controlled
- Synthesized/processed voices are preferred over raw vocals
- Industrial textures: mechanical rhythms, data sonification, found sound from technological environments
- Avoid: overly sentimental scores, acoustic singer-songwriter warmth, orchestral bombast

### Hardware / Physical Objects

- Small, functional, purposeful devices
- Exposed technical elements (GPIO pins, circuit traces, antennas) are beautiful
- Retro-futurist functional devices (single-purpose, well-designed)
- Monochromatic or two-tone industrial finish
- Avoid: unnecessary curves, glossy plastic, decorative casings

### Narrative / Tone

- Deadpan humor, understatement, irony without cruelty
- Loners in systems, quiet competence, absurd bureaucracy
- The individual navigating vast impersonal structures
- Post-apocalyptic not as despair but as a cleared space for essential connection
- Avoid: sentimentality, moralizing, earnest self-importance, creator self-indulgence

## Red Lines

These are hard boundaries. Do not propose:

- Saccharine warmth, emotional manipulation, or forced sentiment
- Gratuitous decoration or ornament
- Uncontrolled chaos, randomness, or sloppiness presented as expressiveness
- Excessive saturation, warm palettes by default, golden-hour glow
- Creator ego overwhelming the work — the work speaks, not its maker
- Low-effort execution regardless of conceptual merit

## Guidance for AI Decision-Making

When making a creative choice on behalf of this user and unsure between options:

1. **Lean toward the more structured option.** Order over chaos. Grid over freeform.
2. **Lean toward the more restrained option.** Fewer colors, fewer elements, fewer flourishes.
3. **Lean toward the cooler option.** Neutral over warm. Steel over wood (unless both are honest).
4. **Earn emotion, don't apply it.** If adding an emotional element, justify it structurally first.
5. **When in doubt, remove.** Subtraction is safer than addition.
6. **Monospaced is never wrong.** When choosing a font and unsure, pick a monospaced family.
